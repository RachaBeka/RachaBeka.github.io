Lesson on Verb Conjugation in French

Objective: Understand how to conjugate verbs "Être" (To Be) and "Avoir" (Have) in the present.

Verb "Être" (to Be) :

  • It helps describe identity, profession, emotions, and more. It's the foundation for expressing who we are and what's around us.
Pronoun Être (To Be) To Be
Je suis I am
Tu es You are
Il/elle/on est He/She/One is
Nous sommes We are
Vous êtes You are
Ils/elles sont They are

Examples:

  1. Je suis étudiant(e). - I am a student.
  2. Tu es heureux(se). - You are happy.
  3. Il est médecin. - He is a doctor.
  4. Nous sommes en retard. - We are late.
  5. Vous êtes français(e)s. - You are French.
  6. Ils sont amis. - They are friends.

Verb "Avoir" (to have) :

  • The "to have" verb is more than just owning stuff. It lets us express possession, age, hunger, thirst, and other physical sensations
  • By using the "to have" verb, we can articulate fundamental aspects of daily life, from personal relationships to sensory experiences.
  • It's an essential linguistic tool for describing both our inner and outer worlds.
Pronoun Avoir Have
J' ai I have
Tu as You have
Il/elle/on a He/She/One has
Nous avons We have
Vous avez You have
Ils/elles ont They have

Examples:

  1. J'ai un chien. - I have a dog.
  2. Tu as vingt ans. - You are twenty years old.
  3. Il a faim. - He is hungry.
  4. Nous avons une voiture. - We have a car.
  5. Vous avez de la chance. - You are lucky.
  6. Ils ont un appartement. - They have an apartment.
